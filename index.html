Research

My research interest lies in the applications of behavioral economics.

Algorithmic Personalization and Digital Addiction: A Field Experiment on Douyin (TikTok) Working Paper

With Qianyun Gao, Yu Gao, and Juanjuan Meng

Powered by algorithmic personalization recommendations, social media has dominated our leisure time and shaped our thoughts. Using a field experiment with Douyin, this paper investigates how deactivating personalized recommendation system or completely stopping using Douyin affect subsequent use time and users’ well-being. We find that both treatments reduce Douyin use during the intervention period relative to the Control. Furthermore, post-intervention persistence is detected in the personalization deactivation group but not in the Douyin abstinence group. Additional evidence indicates that deactivating personalization significantly increases participants’ perceived cognitive capabilities such as self-control and efficiency, while Douyin abstinence decreases their subjective well-being. Our results provide support for algorithmic personalization related policies in digital service.
